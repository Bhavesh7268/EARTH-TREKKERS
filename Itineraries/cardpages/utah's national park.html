Utah Mighty 5 Travel Guide and Road Trip Itinerary

How to visit all five Utah national parks in one epic road trip

Utah's "Mighty 5" national parks - Arches, Canyonlands, Capitol Reef, Bryce Canyon, and Zion - contain some of the most spectacular landscapes in the American Southwest. This road trip itinerary shows you how to visit all five parks in 7-10 days.

Utah Mighty 5 Overview

  • Distance: ~900 mile loop from Las Vegas or Salt Lake City
  • Duration: 7-10 days (minimum)
  • Best Time to Visit: April-May & September-October
  • Major Parks: Zion, Bryce Canyon, Capitol Reef, Canyonlands, Arches
  • Entry Fees: $35/vehicle per park or $80 America the Beautiful Pass

Map of Utah's Mighty 5 national parks

The Mighty 5 National Parks

1

Zion National Park

Famous for its towering sandstone cliffs and the Narrows hike through the Virgin River.

Don't Miss: Angels Landing, The Narrows, Observation Point

2

Bryce Canyon National Park

Known for its unique hoodoos - spire-shaped rock formations.

Don't Miss: Sunrise Point, Navajo Loop, Queen's Garden Trail

3

Capitol Reef National Park

Features the Waterpocket Fold, a 100-mile long wrinkle in the earth's crust.

Don't Miss: Hickman Bridge, Capitol Gorge, Fruita orchards

4

Canyonlands National Park

Massive canyons carved by the Colorado River into three distinct districts.

Don't Miss: Mesa Arch, Grand View Point, Island in the Sky

5

Arches National Park

Contains over 2,000 natural stone arches, including the famous Delicate Arch.

Don't Miss: Delicate Arch, Double Arch, Landscape Arch

7-Day Utah Mighty 5 Itinerary

This fast-paced itinerary covers all five parks in one week:

Day 1: Arrive in Las Vegas, Drive to Zion

  • Drive from Las Vegas to Springdale (2.5 hours)
  • Afternoon hike in Zion (Emerald Pools or Watchman Trail)
  • Overnight in Springdale

Day 2: Zion National Park

  • Hike Angels Landing or The Narrows
  • Drive to Bryce Canyon (2 hours)
  • Overnight near Bryce

Day 3: Bryce Canyon National Park

  • Sunrise at Sunrise Point
  • Hike Navajo Loop and Queen's Garden
  • Drive to Capitol Reef (2.5 hours)
  • Overnight in Torrey

10-Day Utah Mighty 5 Itinerary

With extra time, you can explore more in each park:

Extra Time in Zion
  • Full-day Narrows hike
  • Observation Point via East Mesa Trail
  • Kolob Canyons area
Extra Time in Canyonlands
  • Explore the Needles District
  • White Rim Road (4WD required)
  • Upheaval Dome

Essential Road Trip Tips

Driving
  • Rental car or RV recommended
  • Long distances between services
  • Download offline maps
Camping
  • Reserve campsites early
  • Many first-come, first-served sites
  • BLM land offers free camping
Hiking
  • Carry plenty of water
  • Wear sturdy hiking shoes
  • Check weather for flash floods

Best Time to Visit Utah's National Parks

Season Weather Crowds
Spring (Apr-May) Mild temps, wildflowers Moderate
Summer (Jun-Aug) Very hot, monsoon rains Very crowded
Fall (Sep-Oct) Pleasant temps Moderate
Winter (Nov-Mar) Cold, snow at higher elevations Light (except holidays)

Where to Stay on Your Road Trip

Accommodation options near each national park:

Zion
  • Springdale (many hotels)
  • Watchman Campground
  • Zion Lodge (in park)
Bryce Canyon
  • Bryce Canyon City
  • North Campground
  • Ruby's Inn
Moab (Arches/Canyonlands)
  • Many hotels and campgrounds
  • Devils Garden Campground
  • BLM land for free camping

Final Thoughts

A road trip through Utah's Mighty 5 national parks is an unforgettable adventure through some of America's most stunning landscapes. Whether you have one week or two, this itinerary will help you experience the best of each park, from Zion's towering cliffs to Arches' delicate stone formations.

Park Pass Tip

If you're visiting multiple parks, the $80 America the Beautiful Pass pays for itself and covers all national park entrance fees for one year.
